RAW_SALES = ['Order Number', 'Line Item', 'Order Date', 'Delivery Date',
       'CustomerKey', 'StoreKey', 'ProductKey', 'Quantity', 'Currency Code']

### 🧹Cleaning Steps on RAW_SALES data🧹
- Trimmed text columns and converted to Title case.
- No duplicate rows; only Delivery Date had missing values.
- Converted Order Date and Delivery Date from object to datetime type.
- Filled missing Delivery Date with Order Date + 7 days as a best-estimate.
- Added a flag column to mark imputed delivery dates (useful for ML or analysis).

## Observations on Raw_sales
Currency Code - most sales are in USD, but a significant portion comes from Europe and other regions.
Quantity - Most products are sold in USD, reflecting the dominance of the US market, while Europe and other regions contribute smaller portions of total quantity.
         - Most customers buy 1–3 units per order (~68%), showing that purchases are generally small and individual-focused.
         - Medium to large orders (4–10 units, ~32%) are less common, indicating bulk or wholesale purchases are minimal.
Order date - Orders grew each year from 2016 to 2019, peaking at 21,611.
           - In 2020 and 2021, the number of orders declined noticeably due to Covid.
Line Item - Most of orders come from the top three line items, making up nearly 85% of total orders.
	  - This means we rely heavily on a few key segments for volume.
          - The remaining items have much lower order counts and may need attention to grow or optimize.
